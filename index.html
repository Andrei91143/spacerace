History of Cold War

The Space Race was a big competition between the US and the Soviet Union from 1957 to 1969. It started when the Soviets shocked everyone by launching Sputnik 1, the first man-made satellite, which started a huge rivalry.

Both countries raced to hit major goals in space exploration. For example, the USSR sent the first guy into space, Yuri Gagarin, in 1961. Then in 1969, the Americans won the race by landing the first people on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission.

This competition made science and technology advance really fast. It was also a big way for both sides to prove that their government and way of life were better during the Cold War.

Your mission is to safely fly a space capsule through dangerous space junk, just like the early astronauts had to do, and land on the Moon to win the race for your country.

Space Race Timeline

  • 1957: USSR launches Sputnik 1, first artificial satellite.
  • 1961: Yuri Gagarin (USSR) becomes first human in space.
  • 1962: John Glenn (USA) orbits Earth.
  • 1965: First spacewalk by Alexei Leonov (USSR).
  • 1966: Luna 9 (USSR) first soft lunar landing.
  • 1969: Apollo 11 (USA) lands first humans on the Moon.
